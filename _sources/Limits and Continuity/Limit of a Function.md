# Limit of a Function

````{prf:proposition}
:label: pro:2

Let $f$ and $g$ be complex-valued functions defined on a subset $A$ of a metric space $(X, d)$, and $p$ a limit point of $A$. If $\lim_{x \to p} f(x) = 0$ and $g$ is bounded, then

```{math}
\begin{align*}\lim_{x \to p} f(x)g(x) = 0
\end{align*}
```
````
